Salpingo-Oophorectomy & BRCA Genetics

A Comprehensive Guide to Preventive Health

1. Surgical Overview

Salpingo-oophorectomy is the surgical removal of the fallopian tubes and ovaries.

Unilateral

Removal of one ovary and tube. Typically preserves hormonal balance and fertility.

Bilateral (BSO)

Removal of both. Triggers surgical menopause but drastically reduces cancer risk.

2. How it Prevents Cancer

  • Ovarian Cancer: Removes the primary site of origin (especially the fimbriae of the tubes).
  • Breast Cancer: By removing ovaries, estrogen/progesterone levels drop. This "starves" hormone-sensitive tumors.

3. Understanding BRCA 1 & 2

These are Tumor Suppressor Genes. Everyone has them, but their function changes if they are mutated.

Condition Gene Function Cancer Risk
Normal Repairs DNA breaks efficiently. Average (Baseline)
Mutated DNA "typos" accumulate over time. High (Significantly elevated)
The "Two-Hit" Hypothesis:

You inherit one "broken" copy. If the second healthy copy gets damaged later in life, the cell loses its defense and can become cancerous.
